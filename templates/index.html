Forms of Abuse

Abuse can take many forms beyond physical harm. Understanding these forms helps in recognizing and addressing them early.


Emotional Abuse

Emotional abuse includes repeated humiliation, insults, isolation, or manipulation that gradually affects a person‚Äôs confidence and mental well-being. It may not leave visible marks, but its impact can be deeply distressing.

Threat & Intimidation

Intimidation involves creating fear through threats, pressure, or blackmail to control someone‚Äôs actions or silence them. Even without physical harm, fear-based control is a serious form of abuse.

Economic Abuse

Economic abuse includes controlling access to money, property, employment, or financial resources in order to make a person dependent and powerless.

Physical Abuse

Physical abuse involves the use of force that causes bodily harm, pain, injury, or endangers life. Under Indian law, harming a woman's health, safety, or well-being within a domestic relationship is a punishable offence.

Physical Abuse Case: Study 3

What is Physical Abuse?

Physical abuse involves the intentional use of force that causes bodily pain, injury, disability, or endangers life. Under the Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005, any act that harms a woman‚Äôs health, safety, or well-being is considered an offence.

Common Forms:

  • Slapping, punching, kicking
  • Burning with hot objects or chemicals
  • Strangulation or physical confinement
  • Assault with sharp objects
  • Denial of medical care after injury

Recent Indian Cases:

  • Gurgaon PG Abuse Case (2026): A young student was allegedly confined and severely assaulted by her live-in partner. Read full case ‚Üí
  • Bareilly Dowry Violence Case (2026): A woman was reportedly tortured over dowry demands, leading to serious injuries. Read full case ‚Üí
  • Mahim Harassment Case (Mumbai): Prolonged physical and emotional abuse allegedly led to tragic consequences. Read full case ‚Üí
  • Ghaziabad Elder Abuse Case (2025): CCTV footage showed an elderly woman being physically assaulted inside her home. Read full case ‚Üí

Impact of Physical Abuse:

  • Severe injuries and chronic health problems
  • Depression, trauma, anxiety
  • Breakdown of families and relationships
  • Children exposed to domestic violence may experience long-term psychological trauma
  • Violence within families can create an intergenerational cycle of abuse

Physical violence often begins gradually but can escalate into life-threatening situations.
